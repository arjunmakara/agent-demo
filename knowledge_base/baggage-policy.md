# Wayfare Baggage Policy

## Included Baggage Allowance

Wayfare's package fares include the following baggage allowance per traveler, in addition to whatever the operating airline includes in the base fare:

- **Economy**: 1 personal item + 1 carry-on bag (max 22 x 14 x 9 in / 10 kg), free. 1 checked bag (max 50 lb / 23 kg) is included on itineraries of 5+ nights; on shorter trips a checked bag is a paid add-on (see fees below).
- **Premium Economy**: 1 personal item + 1 carry-on, free. 1 checked bag included on all itineraries regardless of length.
- **Business**: 1 personal item + 1 carry-on, free. 2 checked bags included (each up to 70 lb / 32 kg).

These allowances describe what Wayfare bundles into the package price. The operating airline's own baggage rules always apply as a floor — if an airline's own allowance for your fare class is more generous than the above, the airline's allowance applies instead.

## Additional / Overweight Baggage Fees

- **Extra checked bag** (beyond the included allowance): $60–$90 per bag per direction, depending on route length (domestic vs. international).
- **Overweight bag** (51–70 lb / 23–32 kd): $100 per bag per direction.
- **Oversized bag** (linear dimensions over 62 in / 158 cm): $150 per bag per direction, subject to airline acceptance.
- Bags over 70 lb (32 kg) are not accepted as checked baggage on most carriers and must be shipped as cargo.

Fees above are Wayfare's estimated pass-through of standard airline fees at time of booking; the airline's fee schedule at check-in is authoritative and may differ, especially for last-minute changes.

## Sports Equipment and Special Items

Skis, snowboards, golf clubs, and surfboards can typically be checked in place of one standard bag at no extra charge if within standard weight/size limits, or for a reduced special-item fee ($40–$75) if oversized. Musical instruments too large for the overhead bin generally require purchasing an extra seat or using a cargo service — confirm with the specific airline before booking if this applies to your trip.

## Lost, Delayed, or Damaged Baggage

If checked baggage is lost, delayed, or damaged, report it to the airline's baggage desk at the arrival airport before leaving the airport (this creates a Property Irregularity Report, required for any claim). Wayfare's support team can help you file a claim with the airline and, for travelers with Wayfare Trip Protection, can also help file a supplemental claim for reimbursement of essential items purchased during a baggage delay of 12+ hours (see insurance policy for coverage limits).

## Carry-On Restrictions

Standard carry-on liquid rules apply on all itineraries (containers ≤100 mL / 3.4 oz, fitting in one quart-size bag) unless departing from a country with different regulations, in which case the stricter rule applies. Duty-free liquids purchased after security are generally exempt if sealed in a tamper-evident bag with the receipt visible, but connecting itineraries with a security re-screening may require repacking these into checked luggage.
